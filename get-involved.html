Pick whichever fits.

Four ways in. Some people start with a Sunday circle and never come to a retreat. Some go the other way. There isn't a right order.

Four doors

Where are you?

“I just want to see what this is.”

Come to a Sunday circle

We meet online the first Sunday of each month. Ninety minutes. Usually free. No application, no prerequisites, no reading list. Show up, see if it lands.

Support the work

What gifts fund.

We're small. A donation here doesn't go into a marketing budget.

It pays for scholarship seats at retreats. It keeps the Zoom room running for the monthly circles. It covers stipends for the alumni going through facilitator training. Some of it pays Jenny and Forest for the hours they spend on email, applications, and group supervision — hours that used to be unpaid and weren't sustainable.

We're registered as a 501(c)(3). EIN is listed below. Gifts are tax-deductible in the U.S.

$100 · Infrastructure

Keep the lights on

A month of the Zoom room for Sunday circles. Or half an hour of group supervision for the facilitator cohort. Or one applicant's stipend to attend their scholarship interview.

$1,000 · Scholarship

Fund a seat

Covers partial tuition for one person on scholarship at an upcoming retreat. Usually matched with another gift to close the gap. You won't meet the person, but they'll know someone funded them.

$5,000 · Facilitator training

Train someone to carry this

One facilitator's yearlong training cohort fee. That person then runs circles in their own town for years. One gift, a decade of work.

Scholarships

If the money is the barrier, tell us.

We keep a scholarship fund. It comes from donations and from alumni paying forward after their own retreat. If you want to come and can't pay full price, write to us. We can't fund every ask, but we'll tell you honestly what's available.
