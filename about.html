About

Fifteen years of the work. Five years of this form.

This is the story of how C.Lab came to be. Who built it. Who we learned from. What changed in Costa Rica in 2020. It's a small story, told honestly.

Origin Story

Montezuma, 2020.

Forest and Jenny were on the Pacific coast of Costa Rica. Jenny was pregnant. A question landed.

The question was about depth. Forest had been running curriculum work at Wise Up and the Kresser Institute since 2011. Nine years of building programs that reached more people every year. That year, standing on the beach in Montezuma, something shifted. The pull was no longer toward wider. It was toward deeper. Fewer people. Longer with them. More real.

Two years later, the first C.Lab retreat happened. Ten people. Six days. Then another. Then another. Six retreats in by the time you're reading this. About fifty alumni across four countries. Gatherings forming in France and Quebec. A young women's retreat now in its second year.

The work has been word-of-mouth the whole time. Invitation only. This site is the first time we've opened the door to anyone who might be looking for us.

Who Holds It

Forest & Jenny.

Two people. A marriage. Two decades of practice between them.

They teach together because the work asks for it. One person cannot hold what a room of ten needs when things get real. Two can, when they've been practicing this long, and when they live it at home.

Forest · Portrait

Forest Fein

Wise Up · Kresser Institute · Diamond Approach

Forest has been building curriculum since 2011. First at Wise Up, working with teenagers and young adults. Then at the Kresser Institute, designing clinical training programs. Fifteen years of figuring out how to teach the hard stuff so people actually get it in their body, not just their head.

His own practice runs through the Diamond Approach, the integral awakening work of Jennifer Welwood, and a lot of sitting. He teaches the way he practices. Carefully. Without the spiritual bypass that's common in this territory. With a real map of what the passage feels like from the inside.

Jenny · Portrait

Jenny Fein

Therapist · Somatic practitioner · Group facilitator

Jenny is a therapist. Her training runs through somatic work and relational psychology, and she's been facilitating groups for years. What she does in the room is hard to describe. People feel safer than they expected to. Things come up that they've been carrying for decades. The room holds.

She's the reason the retreats don't break open the way a lot of deep work does. Precision with what's tender. A steady attention to the field between people. If the work lands in the body, it lands because Jenny has been tending the ground.

Lineage

Who we studied under.

None of this is ours. We've spent years sitting with teachers, reading the books, doing the practices. This is where the work actually comes from. We name the sources because they deserve naming.

Diamond Approach

A.H. Almaas. Inquiry into essence. A living method of becoming honest with yourself.

Integral Awakening

Jennifer Welwood. Psychological and spiritual maturity held in the same hand.

The Work That Reconnects

Joanna Macy. Grief and gratitude as practice. Love of the living world.

Evolutionary Spirituality

Sri Aurobindo, Teilhard de Chardin, Andrew Cohen. Consciousness understood as in motion.

Psychedelic Research

Chris Bache. Thirty years of careful work on the archetypal dimensions of mind.

Contemplative Practice

S.N. Goenka, Jack Kornfield, Tara Brach. The quiet foundation: sit, breathe, attend.

Depth Psychology

C.G. Jung, Joseph Campbell. Myth and symbol and the shape of the soul.

Timeline

The arc so far.

Fifteen years on a page. Each year we listened for what wanted to come next and built toward that. No master plan. Just this.

2011

Wise Up begins

Forest founds Wise Up. Curriculum for teenagers. A decade of learning how to teach hard things to people who are paying close attention.

2020

The Montezuma question

Jenny is pregnant. They're on the Pacific coast. A question drops in about going deeper instead of wider. The calling becomes undeniable.

2022

First retreat

Ten people. Six days. The 5 Movements emerge as a container, built from what's actually happening in the room rather than from theory.

2024

The work expands

A young women's retreat runs for the first time. Alumni start asking how to carry this home. The first facilitator conversations begin.

2025

Community coheres

Gatherings form in France and Quebec. A network of people practicing this together, across time zones, between retreats. No longer just retreats.

2026

The door opens

This site. The Facilitator Path. A season of letting other people carry the work forward alongside us.
