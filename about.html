About C.Lab

We are building a living field.

C.Lab is the slow, careful work of many hands — grounded in fifteen years of practice, shaped by lineages far older than any of us, and held by people who have walked this ground themselves.

Origin Story

The Montezuma seed.

C.Lab began as a seed dropped in Montezuma, Costa Rica — a moment of deep listening that became a calling.

After fifteen years of curriculum design and youth education through Wise Up, Forest Fein sensed something more was needed. Not wider reach, but deeper transformation. Not more programs, but a living field where people could come home to themselves and to each other.

Six retreats. Fifty alumni. A growing community spanning four countries. A young women's retreat. Gatherings forming in France. A retreat center held on Costa Rica's southern coast. What started as an experiment has become a lifework.

The work has always been relational, always quiet, always invitation-only. This site is the first time we are opening the door a little wider — not to scale, but to let those who belong here find their way in.

The Partnership

Forest & Jenny.

C.Lab is held by a partnership — an alchemy of two distinct gifts meeting in service of the same field.

Forest brings the architecture — the maps, the curriculum, the intellectual and contemplative rigor shaped across two decades of teaching. Jenny brings the ground — the relational safety, the therapeutic depth, the quiet attention that allows the work to actually land in the body.

Forest · Portrait

Forest Fein

Co-Founder · Curriculum & Teaching

Forest has spent fifteen years designing transformational curriculum, first through Wise Up and the Kresser Institute, and now through C.Lab. His work is shaped by long study in the Diamond Approach, integrated awakening, depth psychology, and the evolutionary lineages.

He teaches the way he lives — with care, with precision, and with a deep refusal to settle for the spiritual bypass so common in this territory. What he offers is a map, a practice, and a steady hand across the passage.

Jenny · Portrait

Jenny

Co-Founder · Relational Field & Facilitation

Jenny brings therapeutic training, facilitation experience, and a presence that shapes the safety of the container itself. Her work is the weaving — attending to what arises between people, holding the emotional truth of the room, and making space for the tender edges of transformation.

Where Forest's gift is the structure, Jenny's is the soil. Together, the container can hold what a single teacher could not. This partnership is the reason the work lands as deeply as it does.

Lineage

Standing on shoulders.

C.Lab is not a new invention. It is a synthesis — shaped by traditions and teachers spanning contemplative practice, depth psychology, integral theory, psychedelic research, and relational wisdom. We name our sources because the work belongs to a much longer conversation than our own.

Diamond Approach

A.H. Almaas — inquiry into essence, the unfolding of being.

Integrated Awakening

Jennifer Welwood — the marriage of spiritual and psychological maturity.

The Work That Reconnects

Joanna Macy — grief, gratitude, and the love of the living world.

Evolutionary Spirituality

Sri Aurobindo, Teilhard de Chardin, Andrew Cohen — consciousness in motion.

Psychedelic Research

Chris Bache — the cosmic and archetypal dimensions of mind.

Contemplative Practice

Goenka, Jack Kornfield, Tara Brach — the foundation of sitting, breathing, attending.

Archetypal & Depth Psychology

Joseph Campbell, C.G. Jung — myth, symbol, and the deep structure of the soul.

Timeline

The arc so far.

C.Lab did not begin with a plan. It began with a seed, and each year has been an act of listening for what wanted to come next. Here is the shape of the path as it has unfolded.

2011

First curriculum seeds

Forest founds Wise Up, beginning a decade of curriculum design and youth education that would later shape C.Lab.

2020

The Montezuma moment

A quiet passage on Costa Rica's southern coast. A seed drops. The calling becomes undeniable.

2022

First C.Lab retreat

An intimate group of ten. The 5 Movements take shape as a container rather than a theory.

2024

Retreats expand

A young women's retreat. First facilitator conversations begin. Alumni start asking how to carry the work forward.

2025

Community coheres

Gatherings forming across continents. A recognizable field emerges — not a program, but a living network of practice.

2026

The door opens

Website launch. The Facilitator Path opens. A new season of shared stewardship begins.
