The Work

Here's what we actually do.

Three ways in. A six-day retreat twice a year. A free online circle the first Sunday of every month. One-on-one sessions with Forest when something specific is asking to be worked with.

The Framework

The shape of the work.

A map Forest developed across fifteen years of facilitating. Five movements. We walk them in retreats and return to them in gatherings.

Not steps to finish. Not levels to unlock. More like weather we keep moving through. Sometimes in order. Usually not.

I

Awakening

The suspicion that something more real is possible.

II

Conditioning

Seeing the box you've been living in.

III

Reconnection

Body. Heart. Ground. The parts of you that never left.

IV

Contribution

What do you do with what you find.

V

Communion

Living inside the whole thing, on its terms.

Retreats

Six days, group of ten, by application.

The retreat is where the 5 Movements become something you've lived, instead of something you've read.

Six days on the Pacific coast of Costa Rica, at a small retreat center near the ocean. Ten people. We move slowly. Mornings open with a sitting practice. Days hold a mix of teaching, somatic work, and relational practice in pairs and small groups. Meals are shared. There's unstructured time in the forest and at the water.

Everyone applies. We read every application, and every accepted person has a call with someone on the team before they arrive. That's the front door, and it's stayed the same for six retreats.

Zoom Gatherings

First Sunday of every month.

Ninety minutes online. Open to everyone. Usually free.

A short teaching at the top, then a practice we do together, then breakouts in threes and fours so people can actually speak. We close as one group. That's the shape.

Bring a friend. Bring a question. Come if you've been to a retreat, come if you haven't, come if you're still deciding whether any of this is for you.

Sessions

Working directly with Forest.

Sixty or ninety minutes. By video. By inquiry.

People come for a few different reasons. Integration after a retreat. A specific question that won't leave them alone. Practice guidance. A passage they want company through. Sessions happen in short series, not as ongoing therapy. This isn't a substitute for therapy, and Forest will tell you if that's what the moment is actually asking for.
